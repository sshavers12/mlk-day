Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in Chester, Pennsylvania

Welcome to a self-guided, multi-building civic experience. Explore the history, reflect on the legacy, and engage with the community as you walk these grounds.

Begin the Journey

How This Works

This is a walking journey. You will move between physical locations on campus. The experience is non-linear—you may start at any building, but we recommend following the path outlined below.

At each stop, look for QR Codes to unlock videos, historical context, and reflection prompts. This digital guide will serve as your companion.

A. University Place – Webb Room

Arrival & Orientation

Start your journey here. The Webb Room serves as our gathering point for orientation and grounding. Connect with others before proceeding to the historical exhibits.

Campus Movement Guide

This building is standalone. Once you have concluded your orientation, please exit and proceed to Alumni Hall for the next chapter of the experience.

B. Alumni Hall

Segregation-Era Reflection Experience

Step into history. This space hosts our segregation-era exhibit, confronting the reality of "White Only" and "Black Only" spaces. Please proceed with respect and mindfulness.

Reflection Point

How does the architecture of separation shape our community today? Take a moment to scan the QR code in the room to share your thoughts.

Go to Reflection Form

C. Harris Commons

Community Art & Collective Expression

Join hands in creating something new. Harris Commons is dedicated to community art and expression. Use the paints and materials provided to add your voice to the collective canvas.

Look for the "Hands" symbol to find the activity area.
